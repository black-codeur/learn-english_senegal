Adjectives and Adverbs Exercises

Exercise 1: Adjectives

Fill in the blanks with the correct form of the adjective:

  1. He is a very (tall) person.
  2. The weather is (cold) today than yesterday.
  3. That was the (good) movie I've seen.
  4. This road is much (safe) than the other one.
  5. The book is (interesting) than the movie.
  6. He gave a (funny) speech at the wedding.
  7. The (old) he gets, the (wise) he becomes.
  8. She is the (beautiful) girl in the class.
  9. The situation is (bad) than I expected.

Exercise 2: Adverbs

Fill in the blanks with the correct adverb:

  1. She sings very (beautiful).
  2. He ran (fast) to catch the bus.
  3. They work (hard) to finish the project on time.
  4. The teacher spoke (clearly) so that everyone could understand.
  5. The child laughed (happily) at the clown.
  6. She drives (carefully) in the city.
  7. The train arrived (late) today.
  8. He answered the question (correct).
  9. The athlete performed (amazingly) in the competition.
  10. The baby is sleeping (peacefully) in the crib.

Exercise 3: Adjectives and Adverbs

Choose whether to use an adjective or an adverb in the blanks:

  1. He runs (quick/quickly) in the mornings.
  2. She is a (good/well) student.
  3. The soup smells (nice/nicely).
  4. They performed (bad/badly) during the concert.
  5. He spoke (polite/politely) to the guests.
  6. This is a (beautiful/beautifully) painting.
  7. She danced (graceful/gracefully) at the party.
  8. The exam was (easy/easily) than expected.
  9. He looks (angry/angrily) all the time.
  10. The children played (happy/happily) in the garden.
